## Preface

Everything I built starts from one observation: **all price action on every instrument, on every timeframe, can only do three things relative to the prior candle.** That's it. Three things. Once you see it, you cannot unsee it. The complexity the industry sells you — the indicators, the oscillators, the black-box systems — dissolves. What's left is pure, universal price action.

The Strat is not a strategy. It is a **framework for reading what price is actually doing** — and more importantly, what it is capable of doing next.

## 1. The Universal Truth — Three Candle Types

Every candle in history on every chart on every timeframe is one of three types relative to the candle that preceded it. No exceptions.

### Candle 1 — Inside Bar
The current candle's high is **lower** than the prior candle's high, AND the current candle's low is **higher** than the prior candle's low.

Price is contained within the prior candle's range. This represents **consolidation, compression, and indecision.** The market is coiling. Energy is building. A 1 candle is not a trade — it is a setup for what comes next.

### Candle 2 — Directional Bar
The current candle takes out **either** the prior candle's high OR the prior candle's low — but not both.

- **2u (Two Up):** Takes out the prior high only. Bullish directional.
- **2d (Two Down):** Takes out the prior low only. Bearish directional.

This is the market making a statement. One side won. Price is moving.

### Candle 3 — Outside Bar (Broadening Formation)
The current candle takes out **both** the prior candle's high AND the prior candle's low.

A 3 candle means the market has engulfed the entire prior range. Both sides were hit. This is a broadening formation — and it is one of the most powerful signals in The Strat because it opens two actionable levels simultaneously.

## 2. Scenarios — The Combination Language

If every candle is a 1, 2, or 3, then every candle-to-candle relationship is a **scenario** — a two-digit (or multi-digit) combination that tells you exactly what setup you are looking at and what the probabilities are.

### Two-Candle Scenarios

| Scenario | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **1-2u** | Inside bar breaks to the upside. Bullish continuation signal. |
| **1-2d** | Inside bar breaks to the downside. Bearish continuation signal. |
| **1-3** | Inside bar breaks both directions. Broadening. Wait for direction to establish. |
| **2u-2u** | Consecutive up bars. Momentum continuation. |
| **2d-2d** | Consecutive down bars. Momentum continuation. |
| **2u-2d** | Up bar followed by a down bar. Potential reversal. |
| **2d-2u** | Down bar followed by an up bar. Potential reversal. |
| **2-1** | Directional bar followed by an inside bar. Compression after a move. Fuel for the next leg. |
| **3-2u** | Outside bar followed by an up bar. Continuation to the upside after engulfment. |
| **3-2d** | Outside bar followed by a down bar. Continuation to the downside after engulfment. |
| **3-1** | Outside bar followed by an inside bar. Maximum compression after maximum expansion. High-energy setup. |

### Three-Candle Scenarios

The most powerful setups in The Strat involve three candles because they describe a complete sequence of compression, direction, and confirmation — or reversal.

- **2-1-2 Reversal:** A directional candle, followed by an inside candle (compression), followed by a directional candle in the *opposite* direction. This is a reversal setup. The inside bar is the pivot.
- **2-1-2 Continuation:** Same structure, but the final 2 candle continues in the *same* direction as the first. Momentum confirmation after a brief pause.
- **3-1-2:** Outside bar creates two targets. Inside bar compresses. The 2 candle picks a direction and triggers. High probability when aligned with higher timeframe bias.
- **1-2-2 Continuation:** Inside bar breaks direction, then continues. Momentum building from compression.

The scenario language is universal. Apply it to a 1-minute chart or a monthly chart — the logic is identical.

## 3. Time Frame Continuity (TFC)

This is the most important concept in The Strat for filtering high-probability from low-probability setups.

Every timeframe is in one of two states at any given moment:
- **Bullish:** The current candle has taken out (or is holding above) the prior candle's high.
- **Bearish:** The current candle has taken out (or is holding below) the prior candle's low.

When multiple timeframes are aligned in the same direction, you have **Time Frame Continuity.** The more timeframes aligned, the higher the probability of follow-through.

### The Timeframe Stack
From largest to smallest:
Monthly → Weekly → Daily → 4H → 1H → 30m → 15m → 5m → 1m

### Full Time Frame Continuity (FTFC)
When **all** timeframes from monthly down to your execution timeframe are aligned in one direction, you have FTFC — **the highest probability environment The Strat identifies.**

In FTFC to the upside, you only take long setups. You do not fade the trend. You do not look for shorts. You align and execute.

### TFC for Filtering
- A 1-2u setup on the 5-minute chart means little if the daily and weekly are bearish.
- The same 1-2u setup with daily, weekly, and monthly all bullish is a high-conviction trade.

**Always identify the TFC before identifying the setup.** Setups without TFC context are noise. Setups within TFC context are signals.

## 4. Actionable Signals — What Triggers a Trade

In The Strat, a trade is triggered when price **takes out a defined level** — the high or low of a prior candle in your scenario. Not before. Not at the open. Not at a guess.

The trigger is mechanical. Price either takes out the level or it does not.

### The Two Actionable Levels on Every Candle
Every candle has a high and a low. Those are your two levels. When price exceeds the high, a 2u is forming. When price breaks the low, a 2d is forming.

On a 3 candle (outside bar), both levels were already taken. The question becomes which direction price chooses to run from that broadening formation.

### Entry Discipline
- Do not anticipate. Wait for the candle to take out the level.
- A 1 candle setup (inside bar) is not a trade until price breaks one of the two levels. Entering inside the 1 candle is guessing.
- Let price confirm the scenario before committing capital.

## 5. Broadening Formations — The Power of the 3 Candle

The 3 candle (outside bar) deserves special attention. It is the most misunderstood candle in retail trading because most traders see it and feel confused — price went both ways, what does it mean?

It means the market has **two open targets.** It has taken out both sides but has not established sustained direction. This is not confusion — it is maximum potential energy.

Following a 3 candle, look for:
- The close relative to the prior candle's range (does it close bullish or bearish?)
- The subsequent candle type (1, 2, or 3) to determine which direction is being pursued
- TFC alignment to determine which side is higher probability

A 3-1-2 setup following a 3 candle that closes bullish, inside bar compression, then 2u breakout — in bullish TFC — is among the highest probability patterns The Strat produces.

## 6. Multi-Timeframe Pivot Points

Every candle high and low on every timeframe is a potential pivot point. These are not arbitrary lines — they are levels where price reversed and where orders reside.

Prior candle highs and lows on larger timeframes act as significant resistance and support. When a daily candle's high aligns with a weekly candle's high aligns with a monthly candle's high — that is a **multi-timeframe pivot convergence,** and it is an area where significant price reaction is probable.

### Using Pivots in The Strat
- Identify the prior candle highs/lows on the monthly, weekly, and daily.
- Mark these on your chart.
- When your lower-timeframe scenario setup aligns with one of these multi-timeframe pivot levels as a target or a trigger — it is a high-confluence trade.

The market respects these levels because institutions use them. They are not indicators. They are the actual record of where price reversed.

## 7. Relative Strength and Weakness

Not all instruments move equally in the same environment. Within a bullish sector, some stocks are leading — they move first and move furthest. Others lag.

In The Strat, **relative strength** means trading the instrument that is the strongest (in a bullish environment) or the weakest (in a bearish environment).

When the broad market is in bullish TFC, find the sector in bullish TFC, then find the stock in that sector in bullish TFC with the tightest, most compressed 1-candle setup. That is where the highest-probability move originates.

Do not trade the laggard hoping it catches up. Trade the leader.

## 8. No Indicators Required

The Strat uses no indicators. Not because indicators are useless to all people — but because in this framework, **price itself contains all the information needed.**

Indicators are derived from price. They lag. They repaint. They create psychological noise that separates you from reading price directly.

The Strat demands direct price reading:
- What is the candle type? (1, 2u, 2d, 3)
- What is the scenario? (2-1-2, 3-1-2, 1-2u, etc.)
- What is the TFC on each timeframe?
- What are the multi-timeframe pivot levels?

These four questions answered — you have a complete picture. Add an indicator and you have added a filter you do not need.

## 9. The System Is Universal

This is not a system for equities. Or futures. Or forex. Or crypto.

It is a system for **price.** And price on every traded instrument on every timeframe in every market in history is only ever a 1, a 2, or a 3.

The rules do not change by asset class. The timeframe stack adjusts based on what you trade (a day trader's stack differs from a swing trader's), but the logic is identical. A 2-1-2 reversal on crude oil futures is the same logic as a 2-1-2 reversal on Apple stock on the weekly chart.

Learn the framework once. Apply it everywhere.

## 10. Patience and Mechanical Execution

The Strat eliminates subjectivity in pattern identification. Either the candle is a 1, or it is not. Either TFC is aligned, or it is not. Either price takes out the level, or it does not.

What it does not eliminate is the psychological discipline required to:
- **Wait** for TFC alignment before entering
- **Wait** for the level to be taken before triggering
- **Not override** the mechanical signal with an opinion
- **Accept** that not every session produces a qualified setup

The trader who waits for FTFC alignment, identifies a 3-1-2 or 2-1-2 setup at a multi-timeframe pivot, and executes without hesitation when the level is taken — that trader is executing The Strat correctly.

The trader who enters the inside bar before it breaks, or takes a setup against TFC because it "looks good," is trading their opinion. That is not The Strat.

## 11. The Core Checklist

Before every trade, answer these questions in order:

1. **What is the TFC on the monthly, weekly, and daily?** (Bullish, bearish, or mixed?)
2. **Is there a qualified scenario forming on my execution timeframe?** (1-2, 2-1-2, 3-1-2, etc.)
3. **Does the scenario align with the higher timeframe TFC?** (Trading with the larger flow, not against it)
4. **Is there a multi-timeframe pivot at or near the target?** (Confluence or obstacle?)
5. **Has price taken out the trigger level?** (Confirmed — not anticipated)

If all five answers are favorable, execute. If any answer is unfavorable, stand down. The next setup is coming.
