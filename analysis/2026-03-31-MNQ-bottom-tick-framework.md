## The Day in One Paragraph

MNQ opened the March 31 session at 23,112 at 6:00 p.m. ET on March 30. Within four hours, price swept the prior-day low at 23,024.25 and extended 63.75 points below it to a session low of 22,960.5. From that low, price reversed and rallied continuously for the next 22 hours, printing a session high of 23,974 — 394 points above the prior-day high of 23,580 — and closing at 23,915. Total reversal: **1,013.5 points**. The low was the session extreme. Everything after it was delivery.

## Part II — What Was Readable Before the Low Printed

### The Liquidity Map at the March 31 Session Open

- **Upside (buy-side liquidity)**: PDH 23,580, ~470 pts above the open. Open air above PDH for 800+ points. Single dominant pool overhead.
- **Downside (sell-side liquidity)**: PDL 23,024.25, ~90 pts below the open. PWL 23,231 already swept Monday — dead reference. Below PDL: no major named reference within 100 points on the intraday chart.

This is the asymmetric map. One identifiable pool below, one identifiable pool above, 1,400 points of range between them. The session's job was to take both.

**Signal**: Any sweep of PDL 23,024.25 that did not sustain beyond the level was the candidate reversal. The shorter the excursion below, the cleaner the setup.

### The Preceding Supply Exhaustion

Monday March 30 was a one-way distribution day:

- HOD 23,580 at 10:00 a.m. ET (premarket)
- Session close 23,139.75 — 440 points off the high
- Final 4H bar (Mon 2–6 p.m. ET) printed the session low at 23,024.25 and closed at 23,106 — near the low, zero buyer response

Three consecutive 4H bars into the close made lower highs and lower lows, with the final bar closing in its lower third. Monday's sellers had been distributing all day and were fully deployed by the 4:00 p.m. close. The overnight sweep of PDL on March 31 was the continuation of that distribution — and the exhaustion point of it.

**Signal**: When the prior session closes near its low after a full-day distribution, the overnight extension into sell-side liquidity below PDL is a probable exhaustion move, not a new trend leg. Institutional short positions are already on; the sweep is the squaring-out.

### The Depth of the Sweep

Session low 22,960.5 = PDL 23,024.25 − 63.75 points. A sweep of 60–80 points below a named level on a futures index is consistent with liquidity consumption, not structural breakdown. A true bearish continuation from PDL would have produced a 150+ point extension with displacement bars maintaining their lows. March 31 did not. The sweep bar (6–10 p.m. ET) closed 379 points off the low in the same 4H window.

**Signal**: When the sweep bar that takes PDL reverses inside the same 4H window and closes more than half of its range off the extreme, the sweep is distributive of shorts, not accumulative of new ones. The low is done.

### The Demand Zone Created

The 6–10 p.m. ET bar, in S&D terms, is a drop-base-rally on the 4H:

- Drop into 22,960.5 (the spike wick down)
- Base around 23,050–23,112 (the consolidation before the push up, though compressed into the same 4H bar)
- Rally to 23,339.5 (close)

The demand zone from that bar: **22,960.5 (distal) to 23,112 (proximal)** — the session open down to the session low. Extending to the bar's close would widen it, but a tight S&D-style drawing uses 22,960.5 to the bar open 23,112 (151.5-point zone). This became the reference for every pullback that followed.

Price did not revisit that zone for the remainder of the session. The first rally carried more than 200 points off the zone's proximal edge without a meaningful retrace.

**Grade: A−.** The one soft spot is the single-tap entry. There was no double-bottom or marginal new low on the 4H. Price made one low and never looked back. That is a feature, not a flaw, for directional traders, but it means the entry opportunity was narrower — you had one 4H window to be wrong before the move left without you.

Every other condition hit in full. The reclaim-failure grade is the strongest read on the tape: PDL was never tested again for the entire 22-hour delivery. That is not consolidation. That is immediate structural conversion.

## Part IV — Delivery vs. Consolidation Read

The question: once the low printed, how did you know the move would deliver to PDH (and beyond) rather than basing overnight and resolving down later?

Four tells answered that question before cash open the next morning.

### Tell 1 — The First Retest Never Came

Three 4H bars after the session-low bar (covering 10 p.m. ET Monday through 6 a.m. ET Tuesday) held a range between 23,234 and 23,405.75. The entire overnight session stayed more than 270 points above PDL. PDL was not tested. The broken level held from distance.

A consolidation day would have produced at least one dip back toward PDL 23,024.25 to test the sweep. March 31 produced no such test. The market had decided.

### Tell 2 — The Base Was Ascending, Not Flat

Three overnight 4H bars:

- Bar 1 (10 p.m.–2 a.m.): low 23,234
- Bar 2 (2–6 a.m.): low 23,238
- Bar 3 (6–10 a.m.): low 23,238, close 23,577.5 (at the top)

Each bar's low was the same or higher. The overnight range compressed upward, with the third bar breaking out of the range entirely by the London/pre-open window. Flat overnight bases that resolve sideways produce rejected breakout attempts. March 31's base produced a clean upside breakout on the first attempt.

### Tell 3 — The Morning Closed at the High

The 6–10 a.m. ET bar printed 23,577.5 as both the high and the close. Zero upper wick. Four hours of rally ending at the extreme of the window. That is a bar that does not know the word "pause." It is a bar that tells you PDH 23,580 — three ticks above the close — is going to be taken within the next four hours.

A consolidation day would have produced a stalled approach to PDH with a wicked rejection. The March 31 approach was a vertical line into the level.

### Tell 4 — The PDH Break Had a Clean Retest

The 10 a.m.–2 p.m. ET bar (cash session) printed 23,877.25 above PDH, pulled back to 23,377.5, and closed at 23,761.75. The pullback to 23,377.5 was 200 points below PDH — a deep retest from above of the broken level's surrounding FVG / base structure. The close at 23,761.75 was 182 points above PDH.

Deep retests that close above the broken level are the hallmark of trend days. Shallow retests with failed recoveries are the hallmark of fade days. The 23,377 retest-and-reclaim confirmed that the PDH break was structural, not a liquidity grab.

The afternoon bar (2–6 p.m. ET) printed 23,974 and closed 23,904.75. The full delivery above PDH extended 394 points — the market did not stop at the first named reference after converting from short to long.

## Part V — The S&D Read

The reversal produced two useful zones and one displacement gap.

**Demand zone (4H):** 22,960.5 to 23,112. Drop-base-rally on the session-open 4H bar. Never revisited on the session — fresh at the close. Qualifies on all five factors: impulsive departure (379 pts from low to close), tight base (single 4H bar), first-touch (fresh), strong distance traveled (1,013 pts to session high), no return. This is the reference for April futures continuation if price dips back during the following week.

**Broken supply flip:** PDL 23,024.25 traded from sell-side liquidity into support. Anyone short from the afternoon of March 30 who added into the close had cost basis between 23,024 and 23,210. Those shorts became trapped as price rallied above 23,340 and held there overnight. The flip zone from 23,024 to ~23,210 was active resistance-turned-support for the rest of the session and into April 1.

**FVG confluence (not directly verifiable on 4H, likely present on lower timeframes):** The displacement bar from 6 a.m.–10 a.m. ET, moving 272 points from 23,305 to 23,577 in four hours, almost certainly produced one or more bullish FVGs on the 15m and 5m charts. Those would have been the tactical re-entry zones during the NY AM pullback to 23,377. The deep retest to 23,377 suggests an FVG sat near that level — a second entry point for anyone who missed the initial breakout.

## Part VI — Bottom Line

March 31 was a **bottom-tick day** with a clean four-phase structure:

1. **Sweep** — PDL 23,024.25 taken to 22,960.5 in the overnight session-open bar.
2. **Base** — Three overnight 4H bars above 23,234, holding 270+ points off the low.
3. **Breakout** — 6–10 a.m. ET bar closed at the session high, tagging PDH.
4. **Delivery** — PDH broken by 394 points; session closed 70 pts off the session high.

The framework grade is A−. The one condition that did not score full marks was the single-tap entry — no double-bottom, no marginal new low. That narrowed the entry window but did not weaken the reversal. Every subsequent condition scored A, including the strongest read of the day: PDL was never retested.

**Entry opportunities, in priority:**

1. Overnight base breakout at 23,405 (close of the second 4H bar above overnight range) — aggressive, the 10 p.m.–2 a.m. ET window was the earliest signal.
2. London-window continuation above 23,425 (Tuesday 2–6 a.m. base high) — mid-risk entry ahead of the PDH run.
3. Retest of broken PDH at 23,377 during the NY AM pullback — the cleanest risk/reward entry; PDH 23,580 was already tagged and the retest closed 200+ points above.

**Reference demand zone for future sessions:** 22,960.5–23,112 (4H). Fresh at the close.
