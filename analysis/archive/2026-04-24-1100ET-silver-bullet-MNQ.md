## Bottom Line

The chop wasn't chop. It was a textbook sweep-and-reject of the 15m demand zone mapped in this morning's S&D playbook. The mechanical Silver Bullet rules fire independently of context — same-direction ChoCH→BOS inside the window = trigger. But the Grade D filter exists precisely for this setup: **"fights an obvious higher-context wall."** Entry price sat inside a known demand floor. Target sat in open air with no magnet. A discretionary filter before the trigger would have skipped this pair and left capital dry for the back-half bullish sequence.
